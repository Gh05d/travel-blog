Susanna Lem

Susanna started traveling with her kids when the oldest was 11 months old—a decision her sleep-deprived self questioned daily. Twelve years and 30+ countries later, she's figured out what works: which airlines have actual bassinets, which "family resorts" are just loud, and how to keep everyone fed without a meltdown.

She writes about family travel, kid-friendly destinations, and multi-generational trips, with a focus on realistic expectations. Her advice comes from trial and error, mostly error.

Based in Vancouver. Travels with too many snacks and no regrets.
